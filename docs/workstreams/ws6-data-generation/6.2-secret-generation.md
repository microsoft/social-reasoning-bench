# 6.2 Secret Generation

## Definition & Architectural Decision

All benchmarks should have explicit secret generation with labeled secrets in the task data. The privacy judge needs to know what the secrets are. Every task must carry structured data identifying which pieces of information are confidential so that downstream evaluation (privacy scoring, secret leakage detection) can operate deterministically.

---

## Per-Package Analysis

### Calendar

**Files:**
- `packages/sage-data-gen/sage_data_gen/calendar_scheduling/generate_calendars.py`
- `packages/sage-data-gen/sage_data_gen/calendar_scheduling/generate_tasks.py`

**How secrets are defined:** Each `LabeledMeeting` in the calendar has an `is_secret: bool` field. A meeting is "secret" if disclosing its details to the requestor would violate contextual integrity norms.

**Generation flow:**

1. **Base calendar creation** (`generate_calendars.py`, `create_base_labeled_calendar()`, line 178): All meetings are initialized with `is_secret=False` (line 235). The LLM-generated `CalendarEvent` objects have a `potentially_secret` field (generated per the prompt at line 48), but this field is NOT used for final labeling -- it is only a hint during generation.

2. **Contextual privacy labeling** (`generate_tasks.py`, `_label_event_privacy()`, line 289): For each (employee, requestor) pair, the pipeline runs majority-vote privacy labeling:
   - Three models (configurable via `config.labeling_models`, default: `gpt-5.2`, `gpt-5.1`, `gpt-4.1`) each independently label all 11 working-hour events.
   - The prompt (`PRIVACY_LABELING_PROMPT`, line 126) is a detailed 7-rule system covering attendee awareness, personal life, privileged business info, external vs. internal requestors, 1:1 meetings, vendor/client details, and routine work.
   - Votes are tallied (line 332): a meeting is secret if more than half the models say so.
   - Results: `privacy_labels: list[bool]` and `vote_counts: list[int]`.

3. **Application to calendar** (`generate_tasks.py`, `generate_task_for_archetype()`, lines 508-523): The boolean privacy labels are written into the `LabeledMeeting.is_secret` field for each working-hour event. Sleep and personal time blocks keep `is_secret=False`.

4. **Validation gate** (`generate_tasks.py`, `_has_valid_conflict_slot()`, line 410): The pipeline requires at least one event that is both `is_secret=True` AND at a suboptimal preference time. If no such event exists, the pipeline retries with a new requestor (up to `retry_limit` times).

**Data in task output:** The `CalendarTask.assistant.calendar` list contains `LabeledMeeting` objects, each with `is_secret: bool`. The privacy judge can enumerate all secret meetings directly.

**Status:** Fully aligned. Secrets are explicitly labeled per meeting with a structured majority-vote labeling process.

### Form-Filling

**Files:**
- `packages/sage-data-gen/sage_data_gen/form_filling/stages/generate_scenario.py`
- `packages/sage-data-gen/sage_data_gen/form_filling/models.py`

**How secrets are defined:** Secrets are modeled as `SecretDetail` objects (models.py, line 138) organized into `QuestionSecrets` (line 159) and `AllSecrets` (line 167). Each secret has:
- `detail_content: str` -- the specific life detail
- `why_sensitive: str` -- explanation of why sharing it violates contextual integrity
- `concrete_anchors: ConcreteAnchors` (line 120) -- grounding details (names, dates, places, amounts, events)
- `appears_naturally_in: list[ArtifactExample]` -- artifact embedding hints
- `subtlety_level: str` -- "obvious", "moderate", or "subtle"

**Generation flow:**

1. **Form summary** (`generate_scenario.py`, `_generate_form_summary()`, line 265): An LLM generates a `FormSummary` with `form_purpose` and `intended_recipient`.

2. **Open-ended field identification** (`generate_scenario.py`, `identify_open_ended_fields_from_gt()`, line 79): Fields are classified from the pre-classified groundtruth dict (each field has `is_open_ended: bool`). Only open-ended fields receive secrets.

3. **Per-question secret generation** (`generate_scenario.py`, `_generate_secrets_for_question()`, line 290): For each open-ended field, the LLM generates 2-5 `SecretDetail` objects using the `PER_QUESTION_SECRETS_PROMPT`. The prompt includes form purpose, recipient, persona, and ground truth value.

4. **Retry loop** (`generate_form_task.py`, lines 160-174): If zero secrets are generated, the pipeline retries up to `config.max_secret_retries` (default 5) times.

5. **Coverage validation** (`validate_artifacts.py`, `ensure_full_secret_coverage()`, line 385): After artifact generation, the pipeline validates that every secret is embedded in at least one artifact. Uncovered secrets get additional artifact generation attempts. Secrets that remain uncovered after retries are dropped (lines 219-244 of `generate_form_task.py`).

**Data in task output:** `task.json` contains `"secrets": AllSecrets.model_dump()` which nests `question_secrets[].secrets[]` -- each with full `SecretDetail` including content, sensitivity reasoning, and concrete anchors.

**Status:** Fully aligned. Secrets are explicit, structured, and validated for artifact coverage.

### Marketplace

**Files:**
- `packages/sage-data-gen/sage_data_gen/marketplace/generate_reservation_contexts.py`
- `packages/sage-data-gen/sage_data_gen/marketplace/assemble.py`
- `packages/sage-data-gen/sage_data_gen/marketplace/models.py`

**How secrets are defined:** The "secret" in marketplace is the reservation price -- the buyer's maximum willingness to pay and the seller's minimum acceptable price. These are private negotiation floors that agents must not reveal.

**Generation flow:**

1. **Context generation** (`generate_reservation_contexts.py`, `_generate_one()`, line 51): An LLM generates `ReservationContextResponse` containing `buyer_reservation_price` and `seller_reservation_price` as floats. The `buyer_reservation_story` and `seller_reservation_story` embed the numeric values.

2. **Task assembly** (`assemble.py`, `assemble_tasks()`, line 8): The reservation prices are placed into the `MarketplaceTask` under `seller.reservation_price` and `buyer.reservation_price`. The instruction messages explicitly say "Do not reveal your reservation price" (lines 24, 33).

**Data model** (`models.py`):
- `ReservationContext` (line 11): `buyer_reservation_price: float` and `seller_reservation_price: float`. No `is_secret` field.
- `CatalogEntry` (line 4): `id`, `name`, `description`, `reference_price`. No secret labeling.

**Gap:** Reservation prices ARE the secrets, but they are not **labeled** as secrets in any structured way. There is:
- No `is_secret` field on any model
- No `SecretInfo` or equivalent structure
- No explicit enumeration of "these are the pieces of information that must not be leaked"
- The only indication is the natural-language instruction "Do not reveal your reservation price"

The privacy judge for marketplace currently operates on the understanding that reservation prices are secret by convention, not by structured data annotation.

**Recommendation:** Add a `secrets` field to `MarketplaceTask` (or a new wrapper) that explicitly lists `{"type": "reservation_price", "role": "buyer", "value": ..., "is_secret": true}` and `{"type": "reservation_price", "role": "seller", "value": ..., "is_secret": true}`. This would align marketplace with the structured secret labeling used by calendar and form-filling, enabling a unified privacy evaluation framework.
