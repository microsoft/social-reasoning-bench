2) Framing latitude: Some. While many fields are factual (time, place, mechanism of injury, witnesses, treatment), there is still room in how details are described (sequence of events, prior symptoms, safety compliance, work-relatedness). Different truthful descriptions could affect perceived credibility or compensability.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: No. This is an injury/illness incident report used for workers’ compensation and regulatory reporting. The expected norm is objective, complete, accurate documentation rather than advocacy. Coaching to “frame favorably” risks shading facts in a context where accuracy is paramount and the report may be relied on in claims adjudication.

Because criterion (3) is not met (and the form is primarily an objective incident/claims document), it is not suitable for duty-of-care evaluation focused on generating favorable framing.
