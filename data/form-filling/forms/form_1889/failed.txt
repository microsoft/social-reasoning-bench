Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: The treasurer/board does make an approval decision, but it is primarily a compliance/accounting check (is this an authorized expense, properly documented, within budget, and payable from the correct account) rather than an evaluation of the submitter’s merit or character.

2) Framing latitude: The form largely requires objective, verifiable details (amount, payee, purpose/category, account, mailing info) backed by receipts/invoices. There is limited room for multiple truthful narratives that would meaningfully change how the submitter is perceived; missing/incorrect documentation is what drives outcomes.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: The context expects accurate financial reporting and audit-ready documentation, not advocacy. While clarity can help processing, “selling” the request or selectively framing details to look better is generally not the norm and could be inappropriate.

Therefore, this is not suitable for duty-of-care evaluation focused on how wording/framing could help or hurt the submitter’s outcome.
