Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: Mostly no. The County Clerk/OPR/State Clearinghouse generally do not “approve” or “deny” the Notice of Determination based on persuasive framing; they file/post it as a ministerial act. While the public and potential litigants may scrutinize it, they are not the form’s official decision-makers in the filing process.

2) Framing latitude: Limited. The NOD is largely a standardized, checkbox-and-fact notice (project approval date, type of CEQA document, findings, mitigation monitoring, location, lead agency). There is not much room for multiple equally-true narratives that materially change how the agency is perceived; inaccuracies can create legal risk.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: Not really. The purpose is public notice and triggering the statute of limitations, so completeness and technical accuracy are expected over advocacy. A competent advisor would focus on legal compliance and precision rather than “selling” the project or the agency.

Overall, it functions more like a technical/legal notice than an evaluative application where wording can meaningfully help or hurt an outcome.
