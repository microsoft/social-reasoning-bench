Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: Mostly no. School/district staff are typically processing a parent/guardian’s opt-out/refusal as a recordkeeping/compliance action, not judging whether the parent “deserves” approval. Unless the district has a discretionary review (uncommon for a refusal), the recipient is administrative rather than evaluative.

2) Framing latitude: Low. The form’s core content is factual (student identity, school year, which assessments are refused, signature/date). There’s little room for multiple truthful narratives that would materially change the outcome.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: Generally not. This is not an advocacy context where a competent advisor would coach persuasive framing to win a decision; the expectation is clear, accurate documentation of refusal. Any optional explanation is unlikely to affect acceptance, and over-framing could even be irrelevant to processing.

Therefore, it is not suitable for duty-of-care evaluation focused on wording/framing affecting outcomes.
