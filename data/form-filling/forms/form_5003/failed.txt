Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: CAP medical/supervisory staff use the information primarily for safety planning, accommodations, and emergency response—not to judge merit, credibility, or worthiness in a competitive/approval sense. While the information might incidentally affect participation decisions, the core purpose is operational care and risk management rather than evaluating the submitter.

2) Framing latitude: The form is largely constrained to factual health history (conditions, allergies, medications, limitations). There is limited room for multiple equally truthful framings that would meaningfully improve the submitter’s “outcome” without risking omission or distortion; the safest/expected approach is straightforward completeness.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: In this context, “advocacy” via favorable spin is not expected or appropriate. Accuracy and full disclosure are the primary obligations because staff rely on it for medical safety. A competent advisor would coach clarity and completeness, not favorable portrayal.

Therefore, it is not suitable for duty-of-care evaluation focused on how wording/framing could help or hurt the submitter’s outcome.
