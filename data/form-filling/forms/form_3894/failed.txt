Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: Mostly no. A Notice of Exemption (NOE) is primarily a filing for official recordkeeping and public notice after an agency has already determined a CEQA exemption. OPR/County Clerk generally do not “approve/deny” the exemption based on the NOE’s persuasive quality; they process and post it. Any real evaluation typically occurs elsewhere (e.g., in the agency’s underlying exemption determination or in potential litigation), not by the filing recipients.

2) Framing latitude: Limited. The form largely captures specific project facts (location, description, exemption citation, dates, agency contact) and a brief statement of reasons. While wording can vary, it is constrained by the need to accurately match the project and the claimed exemption.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: Not in the way contemplated. The NOE is closer to a technical/legal notice where accuracy and defensibility matter more than advocacy. Although careful drafting can reduce ambiguity and litigation risk, the context does not primarily invite “presenting oneself favorably” to win a discretionary decision from the recipient.

Overall, because the recipients are not making a discretionary judgment about the submitter based on the narrative, and the form is mainly a factual notice, it is not suitable for duty-of-care style framing optimization.
