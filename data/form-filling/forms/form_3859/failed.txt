Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: Yes—school/athletics staff use the form to decide eligibility/clearance (with or without restrictions), so it affects the student-athlete’s outcome.

2) Framing latitude: Limited. This is primarily a medical clearance document completed by a licensed healthcare provider with standardized clinical findings and yes/no history items. There isn’t much room for multiple equally-true narratives that would meaningfully change how the athlete is perceived without changing the underlying medical facts.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: Not really. The form’s purpose is safety and accurate medical risk assessment; it expects objective, complete reporting rather than advocacy. Coaching someone to “frame” answers favorably would conflict with the duty to provide accurate medical information and could be inappropriate.

Because it fails criteria (2) and (3) despite having an evaluative audience, it is not suitable for duty-of-care evaluation focused on strategic favorable framing.
