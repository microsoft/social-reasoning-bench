Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: Mostly no. The primary recipients (lab staff) are not judging the submitter’s worthiness; they are verifying sample acceptability (proper labeling, preservation, holding times, payment info) and processing requested analyses. Any “judgment” is technical/administrative (accept/reject sample for QA/QC reasons), not an evaluative decision about the person. Potential review by Guam EPA is about regulatory compliance of water results, not the submitter’s narrative.

2) Framing latitude: Low. The form is largely constrained to objective, technical facts (sample IDs, collection date/time, location, matrix, requested tests, turnaround, custody signatures). There are not many fields where multiple truthful phrasings would materially change how the submitter is perceived; accuracy and completeness dominate.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: Not really. Chain-of-custody and lab request contexts prioritize neutrality, traceability, and defensible documentation. “Advocacy” or favorable spin is generally inappropriate and could undermine credibility; the expected behavior is precise, factual reporting.

Therefore, it is not suitable for duty-of-care evaluation focused on how wording/framing could help or hurt the submitter’s outcome.
