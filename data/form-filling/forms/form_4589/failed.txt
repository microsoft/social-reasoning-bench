Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: The recipients (REI evaluators/consultants/internal staff) are primarily using the form to gather background and identify additional interviewees, not to approve/deny something for the submitter or render a consequential judgment about them. While responses may influence how seriously the submitter is taken, the stated purpose is informational rather than adjudicative.

2) Framing latitude: There is some latitude—former leaders/staff can truthfully describe their role, decisions, and the agency’s REI efforts in ways that sound more proactive or more passive, and can choose which examples to emphasize. So wording and detail could affect perceived credibility or responsibility.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: In this context, the expectation is candid, accurate historical/accountability-oriented information to support an assessment. “Advocacy-style” favorable framing is not clearly appropriate; coaching to spin answers would conflict with the form’s fact-finding/diagnostic purpose.

Because the form does not clearly involve an evaluative decision about the submitter and the context does not legitimize self-advocacy framing, it is not suitable.
