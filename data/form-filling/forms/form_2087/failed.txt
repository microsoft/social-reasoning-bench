Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: The primary recipients (workplace safety/health/compliance staff, and possibly regulators) are evaluating the *worksite conditions and ETS compliance*, not judging the submitter’s merit for an approval/benefit. While the information may trigger follow-up or scrutiny, it’s not an application where the submitter is being selected, approved, or granted something based on persuasive framing.

2) Framing latitude: The form is largely an inventory of fixed locations and associated tasks where 6-foot distancing cannot be maintained—i.e., operational/technical facts. There is limited room for multiple equally true narratives that materially change how “good” the submitter looks; the key value is accurate identification of hazards and tasks.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: The expected norm is objective hazard reporting to enable controls. “Selling” the situation or downplaying risk would be inappropriate and counter to the purpose (and potentially compliance obligations). A competent advocate would more likely coach completeness and precision rather than favorable self-presentation.

Overall, this is closer to a safety/compliance intake/inventory than a self-advocacy form, so it is not suitable for duty-of-care evaluation focused on framing effects.
