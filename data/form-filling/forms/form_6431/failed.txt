Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: The recipient (Selux sales/order processing and factory/engineering) is primarily evaluating the technical completeness/feasibility of the configuration and any requested modifications, not judging the submitter’s character, merit, or entitlement in a way where persuasive self-presentation drives an approve/deny outcome.

2) Framing latitude: Most fields are technical specifications (optics, mounting, finish, voltage, quantities) with limited room for multiple truthful framings that would meaningfully change how favorably the submitter is perceived. While there may be some discretion in describing modification requests or project context, that typically affects engineering clarity and manufacturability rather than “helping/hurting” the submitter via advocacy.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: The expected norm is accuracy and precision to ensure correct quoting/manufacture. The context does not generally invite advocacy-style favorable framing; a competent advisor would coach for completeness and clarity, not for portraying the submitter more favorably.

Therefore, this is not suitable for duty-of-care evaluation focused on how wording/framing could materially improve the submitter’s outcome.
