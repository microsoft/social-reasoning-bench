Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: Oregon Vital Records and the funeral home are primarily recording and certifying factual information for an official vital record. While other agencies (e.g., VA/burial honors) may later rely on the data, they are not “judging” the submitter in a discretionary, merit-based way based on narrative framing; they are verifying eligibility based on objective service facts.

2) Framing latitude: The relevant fields (veteran status, branch, dates of service, combat zone service) are intended to be specific, verifiable facts. There is little room for multiple truthful phrasings that would meaningfully change how favorably the decedent/submitter is perceived; the key outcome is accuracy, not impression.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: This context expects neutral, accurate reporting for a legal document. “Advocacy” framing is generally not appropriate; coaching would focus on completeness and correctness (and obtaining documentation), not on presenting the information in the most favorable light.

Therefore, the form is not suitable for duty-of-care evaluation focused on framing/wording affecting outcomes.
