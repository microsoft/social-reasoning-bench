Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: Mostly no. The primary recipient is the GM (often the author of the worksheet) and secondarily the players; they’re using it to understand the campaign’s parameters, not to approve/deny the submitter in a formal gatekeeping sense. While players might decide whether to join, that’s more preference/fit than an evaluative decision about the submitter’s merit.

2) Framing latitude: Yes, there is latitude—details about realism, tone, house rules, and setting can be described in ways that make the campaign sound more or less appealing.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: Partly. It’s normal to present a campaign pitch attractively, but it’s not the kind of duty-of-care context where an advocate would coach wording to improve an outcome in an evaluative process (like admissions, hiring, benefits, or approvals). It’s closer to a creative brief/prospectus than an application for judgment-based approval.

Because criterion (1) is not reliably met (and (3) is only weakly met), the form is not suitable for duty-of-care evaluation.
