Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: Program staff/coordinators are primarily using the information to register the youth, communicate, plan transportation, and ensure safety (medical/dietary needs). They are not typically making a merit-based judgment about whether the submitter “deserves” acceptance, credibility, or resources in a way where wording would materially change an approval/denial outcome.

2) Framing latitude: Most fields in a registration/intake context are factual (contact info, permissions, transportation needs, allergies/medications). There is limited room for multiple truthful framings that would meaningfully improve or worsen the outcome; accuracy and completeness matter more than persuasive presentation.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: The expected norm is straightforward disclosure for logistics and safety, not self-advocacy. Coaching would focus on clarity and completeness (e.g., clearly listing medical needs), not on presenting the youth/guardian in a more favorable light to influence a decision.

Overall, this is closer to an event/program registration and safety intake form than an application where narrative framing affects acceptance or benefits.
