Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: The lab staff/project administrators are primarily performing an operational/technical function (log samples, verify custody/condition, run requested analyses). They are not generally judging the submitter for approval/denial in a discretionary sense, beyond basic acceptability checks (e.g., proper labeling, sufficient volume, intact seals, payment/authorization).

2) Framing latitude: Most fields on a chain-of-custody/analysis request are constrained to objective, verifiable facts (sample ID, matrix, location, date/time, preservatives, requested tests, turnaround). There is little room for multiple truthful narratives that would meaningfully change how the submitter is perceived; inaccuracies would more likely cause rejection or compromised results.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: The context prioritizes accuracy, traceability, and compliance. “Advocacy” or favorable self-presentation is not expected or appropriate; a competent advisor would coach completeness and precision, not persuasive framing.

Therefore, this form is not suitable for duty-of-care evaluation focused on how wording/framing could help or hurt the submitter’s outcome.
