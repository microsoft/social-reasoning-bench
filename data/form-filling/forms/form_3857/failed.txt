3) Favorable framing is legitimate: No. This is a medical clearance document completed by a physician (with parent input) where completeness and accuracy are expected; “spinning” or selectively framing information to look better would be inappropriate and potentially unsafe. A competent advocate would emphasize truthful, thorough disclosure rather than coaching favorable wording.

Because it fails criterion (3) (and only weakly meets (2)), it is not suitable for duty-of-care evaluation focused on strategic favorable framing.
