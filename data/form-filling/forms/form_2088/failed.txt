Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: The primary recipients (EHS/infection prevention/occupational health, supervisors) are using the information to identify hazards and plan controls, not to judge the submitter’s worthiness for approval/denial. While regulators/auditors may review it, that review is aimed at compliance and accuracy of hazard documentation rather than evaluating the submitter in a discretionary, character-based way.

2) Framing latitude: The form is largely an inventory of tasks, locations, and exposure scenarios (including aerosol-generating procedures). Those are mostly factual/technical descriptions with limited room for multiple equally-true portrayals that would meaningfully improve the submitter’s outcome.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: In this context, the expectation is objective, complete hazard reporting. “Favorable framing” (downplaying exposures, omitting risky tasks) would be inappropriate and could undermine safety/compliance; a competent advisor would coach for accuracy and completeness, not advocacy.

Overall, it functions as a safety/hazard documentation tool rather than an evaluative application where strategic self-presentation is expected.
