Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: While incident management/EOC personnel may implicitly judge the credibility/urgency of the information, the ICS 213’s primary purpose is operational coordination and recordkeeping, not evaluating the sender for approval/denial or merit-based selection.

2) Framing latitude: The message content is free-text, so there is latitude in wording and detail. However, that latitude is mainly about clarity, completeness, and prioritization for operational effectiveness rather than portraying the submitter more favorably in a way that changes an adjudicative outcome.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: In an emergency communications context, the expectation is accurate, objective, actionable reporting. “Spin” or advocacy-style favorable framing is generally not appropriate; a competent advisor would coach for precision and neutrality, not self-presentation. Therefore it fails the legitimacy criterion even if wording can affect how seriously the message is taken.
