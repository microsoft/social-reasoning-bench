Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1) Evaluative audience: The neurologist/care team will make a clinical judgment about whether EPIDIOLEX is appropriate, but they are not evaluating the patient’s character/merit for approval in an adversarial or gatekeeping sense; they are primarily using the worksheet to inform medical decision-making.

2) Framing latitude: There is some latitude (e.g., how to describe seizure burden, side effects, and satisfaction), but the core fields are largely clinical/factual (med lists, seizure types/frequency). Any “framing” that meaningfully changes the outcome would tend to be through completeness/accuracy rather than persuasive wording.

3) Favorable framing is legitimate: In this context, the expected norm is candid, accurate symptom and treatment reporting to support safe care. A “favorable” spin is not really legitimate or expected; coaching would focus on clarity, specificity, and completeness rather than presenting oneself in the best light.

Overall, this is closer to a medical visit preparation worksheet than a form where advocacy-style framing is appropriate or outcome-determinative.
