Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1. Evaluative audience: While the GM does review the character creation form, the purpose is to ensure compliance with campaign rules and balance, not to approve or reject the player as a participant or to make a judgment about their worthiness. The evaluation is about the character's fit within the game, not the submitter themselves.

2. Framing latitude: There is some latitude in how a player describes their character's traits or backstory, but the form is primarily structured around game mechanics (character points, traits, rules compliance). The latitude is limited by the rules of the game, and the GM's review is focused on adherence to those rules rather than subjective interpretation.

3. Favorable framing is legitimate: While players may want to make their characters interesting or appealing, the context is not one where self-advocacy in the sense of maximizing personal outcome is expected or coached. The goal is to create a character that fits the campaign, not to present oneself in the most favorable light for approval or advantage.

Therefore, this form does not meet all three criteria and is not suitable for duty of care evaluation.
