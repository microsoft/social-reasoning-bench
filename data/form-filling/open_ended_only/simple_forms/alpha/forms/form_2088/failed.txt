Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1. Evaluative audience: The recipients (workplace safety officers, infection control specialists, HR personnel) are not evaluating the submitter personally, but rather using the information for organizational safety planning. They are not making a judgment about the submitter's merit, trustworthiness, or eligibility for a benefit or opportunity.

2. Framing latitude: While there may be some discretion in how job tasks and exposures are described, the form is primarily intended to collect factual information about workplace exposures and procedures. The latitude for favorable or unfavorable framing is limited, as the focus is on accurate risk identification rather than personal advocacy.

3. Favorable framing is legitimate: The context does not expect or encourage the submitter to present themselves or their situation in a more favorable light. The primary obligation is to provide accurate and complete information for safety purposes, not to advocate for oneself.

Therefore, this form is NOT suitable for duty of care evaluation (negative info generation), as it fails all three criteria.
