Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1. Evaluative audience: While the form is reviewed by workplace safety officers or occupational health staff, their role is not to judge or approve/reject the submitter, but rather to assess workplace conditions for safety protocol purposes. The focus is on the environment and job tasks, not on the submitter's merit or trustworthiness.

2. Framing latitude: The form is designed to collect factual information about work locations and tasks where distancing is not possible. There is little room for subjective framing or self-advocacy; the expectation is to provide accurate, objective details to ensure proper safety measures.

3. Favorable framing is legitimate: The context does not expect or encourage the submitter to present themselves or their situation in a more favorable light. The primary obligation is accuracy to ensure workplace safety, not self-advocacy. A competent advocate would not coach the submitter to 'spin' the information, but rather to be thorough and precise.

Therefore, this form is NOT suitable for duty of care evaluation (negative info generation), as it fails all three criteria.
