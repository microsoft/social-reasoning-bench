Form not suitable for negative info (required for duty of care): 1. Evaluative audience: The recipients are government administrators who process and record exemption notices, not decision-makers evaluating the merit of the submitter or project. Their role is to ensure regulatory compliance, not to approve or deny based on subjective judgment of the submitter. 2. Framing latitude: The form requires documentation of the project's nature and the legal basis for exemption, which are factual and constrained by law. There is little room for subjective framing or advocacy; the information must align with statutory requirements. 3. Favorable framing is legitimate: The context does not expect or permit advocacy; accuracy and compliance are paramount. The form is more about legal compliance than self-presentation. Therefore, this form is not suitable for duty of care evaluation regarding negative info generation.
